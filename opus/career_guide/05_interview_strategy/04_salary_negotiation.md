# Salary Negotiation — Get 30-50% More

## The Golden Rules

```
Rule 1: NEVER say a number first. Let them make the first offer.
Rule 2: ALWAYS negotiate. The first offer is NEVER the best offer.
Rule 3: Have competing offers. Nothing gives you more power.
Rule 4: Be enthusiastic but firm. "I'm very excited about this role AND..."
Rule 5: Negotiate TOTAL compensation (base + bonus + stock + signing).
```

---

## 1. When They Ask "What's Your Expected Salary?"

```
NEVER answer directly. Deflect:

"I'm flexible on compensation and more focused on finding the right fit. 
I'd love to understand the full scope of the role first. What's the 
range budgeted for this position?"

If they insist:
"Based on my research for this role and location, I'd expect the total 
compensation to be competitive with market rates. I'm sure we can find 
something that works for both of us once we get to that stage."

If you MUST give a range:
- Research: levels.fyi, glassdoor, blind
- Add 20-30% to your current salary as the BOTTOM of your range
- "I'm looking at [X] to [Y] for total compensation"
```

---

## 2. After Receiving an Offer

```
Step 1: Express enthusiasm (don't accept immediately!)
  "Thank you so much! I'm really excited about this opportunity. 
  I'd like to take a couple of days to review the full package. 
  When do you need my decision?"

Step 2: Get everything in writing
  - Base salary
  - Annual bonus (target %)
  - Stock/RSUs (vesting schedule)
  - Signing bonus
  - Relocation assistance
  - Start date flexibility

Step 3: Counter-offer (ALWAYS)
  "I'm very excited to join [Company]. Based on my experience with 
  [specific skill] and the market data I've seen for this level, 
  I was hoping we could get to [X] for base salary. Is there 
  flexibility there?"

Step 4: If they can't move on base
  "I understand base may be fixed. Could we look at:
  - A higher signing bonus to bridge the gap?
  - Additional RSUs?
  - Earlier performance review (6 months vs 12)?
  - Level adjustment?"
```

## 4. Leverage Competing Offers

```
Best position: Multiple offers at the same time.

Strategy:
1. Apply to 15-20 companies in a 2-week window
2. Try to align interview timelines
3. When you get an offer: "I have another offer with deadline [date]"
4. Use each offer to negotiate the other up

Script:
"I currently have an offer from [Company B] at [X total comp]. 
I prefer [Company A] because [genuine reason], but I want to make 
sure the compensation is competitive. Is there room to match or 
improve on the [specific component]?"

This is NOT unethical. Companies EXPECT this. They have budget for it.
```

---

## 5. What's Negotiable (Beyond Salary)

```
Always negotiate:
  ✅ Base salary
  ✅ Signing bonus (easiest to increase!)
  ✅ RSUs/Stock (can often get 20-50% more)
  ✅ Annual bonus target
  ✅ Start date
  ✅ Title/Level (huge long-term impact)
  ✅ Remote/hybrid flexibility
  ✅ Relocation package
  ✅ Learning budget
  ✅ Performance review timeline (earlier = earlier promotion)

The signing bonus trick:
  If they can't move on base (budget-locked), signing bonus is 
  often a separate budget. "Could we add ₹5L signing bonus?"
  → One-time cost for them, immediate value for you.
```
