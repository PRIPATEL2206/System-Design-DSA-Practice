# Interview Day Protocol — The 5-Minute Read

## The Night Before

```
□ Sleep 8 hours (non-negotiable — your brain solves problems while sleeping)
□ Lay out clothes (something comfortable and professional)
□ Test setup: camera, mic, internet, IDE (if virtual)
□ Charge laptop and phone fully
□ Set 2 alarms (phone + backup)
□ Light dinner (no heavy food, no alcohol)
□ 30 min max review — just skim cheatsheets, don't study new things
□ Positive self-talk: "I'm prepared. I've put in the work."
```

---

## Morning Of

```
□ Wake 2 hours before first round
□ Breakfast: Protein + complex carbs (eggs, oats, fruit — not just chai)
□ 10 min walk or stretch (gets blood flowing to brain)
□ Quick shower (freshens you up)
□ 15 min: Skim DSA patterns cheatsheet (01_dsa_final_revision.md)
□ 10 min: Skim system design one-pager
□ STOP studying 30 minutes before interview
□ 5 min: Deep breaths, power pose, smile in mirror

DO NOT:
  ✗ Try to solve a hard LeetCode problem (will shake confidence if stuck)
  ✗ Read anything new
  ✗ Check social media (negative content affects mood)
  ✗ Skip breakfast (brain needs glucose)
```

---

## 5 Minutes Before Interview

```
□ Glass of water on desk
□ Notepad + pen ready
□ Close all unnecessary tabs/apps
□ Open IDE/code editor (blank file ready)
□ Open calculator (for estimation)
□ Mute phone
□ Take 3 deep breaths (4 count in, 7 hold, 8 out)
□ Smile. Yes, literally smile. It changes your brain chemistry.

Mantra: "I am a competent engineer. I've solved harder problems than this.
         This is a conversation between two engineers."
```

---

## During Each Round

### First 30 Seconds (CRITICAL)
```
- Smile genuinely
- "Hi [name], great to meet you! I'm excited for this conversation."
- Make eye contact with camera (not screen)
- Energy level: Enthusiastic but not manic
```

### Coding Round
```
1. LISTEN to the full problem (don't jump ahead)
2. REPEAT the problem back: "So we need to find..."
3. ASK 2-3 clarifying questions (shows maturity):
   - "What's the expected input size?"
   - "Should I handle edge cases like empty input?"
   - "Can I assume the input is valid?"
4. THINK for 30 seconds silently (this is OK!)
5. STATE your approach before coding
6. CODE while explaining your logic
7. TEST with example from the problem
8. VERIFY edge cases
```

### System Design Round
```
1. CLARIFY: "Let me understand the requirements..."
2. Don't jump to architecture — ask about scale first
3. Draw high-level before diving deep
4. Drive the conversation (don't wait for interviewer to guide)
5. Proactively discuss trade-offs
6. Mention monitoring/alerting (shows production experience)
```

### Behavioral Round
```
1. Use STAR format for EVERY answer
2. Be specific (names, numbers, dates)
3. Always quantify: "reduced by 60%", "served 50K users"
4. Be honest about failures (show learning)
5. Don't badmouth previous employers/colleagues
6. Have 2 questions ready for the interviewer
```

---

## If You Get Stuck (Coding)

```
DON'T: Sit in silence for more than 60 seconds
DON'T: Say "I don't know" and give up
DON'T: Get visibly frustrated

DO:
1. Verbalize: "I'm thinking about approaches..."
2. Go back to examples: "Let me trace through this case..."
3. Think about simpler version: "What if the array was sorted?"
4. Consider brute force: "The naive approach would be..."
5. Ask for a hint gracefully: "Could you point me in the right direction 
   for the optimization?"
```

---

## Between Rounds (5-10 min break)

```
□ Drink water
□ Stand up, stretch, walk briefly
□ DO NOT replay the previous round in your head
□ Reset mentally: "Fresh start. New round. New chance."
□ Quick power pose (arms up, chest open — 30 seconds)
□ Previous round is DONE. Can't change it. Focus forward.
```

---

## After the Interview

```
Same day:
  □ Write down questions you were asked (for future prep)
  □ Note what went well and what to improve
  □ Send thank-you email to recruiter (optional but nice)
  □ Do something relaxing — you earned it!

Next day:
  □ Follow up if recruiter said they'd respond by a certain date
  □ Continue preparing for other companies (don't stop momentum!)
  □ Update your notes on that company's interview style

If rejected:
  □ Ask recruiter for feedback (they sometimes share it)
  □ Note areas to improve
  □ Apply again in 6-12 months (most companies allow re-interview)
  □ Remember: Even top candidates get rejected sometimes.
     Google rejects 99.5% of applicants. It's not personal.
```

---

## Mindset (Most Important)

```
❌ "I need to know everything"
✅ "I need to show how I think and learn"

❌ "If I make a mistake, I'm done"
✅ "Bugs are normal. Finding and fixing them shows skill"

❌ "They're testing if I'm smart enough"
✅ "They're testing if I'd be a good colleague"

❌ "I have to solve it perfectly in 45 minutes"
✅ "I need to show my approach, communicate well, and make progress"

The interview is a CONVERSATION, not an exam.
They WANT you to succeed (empty positions cost them money!).
Show them YOU — your thinking, your energy, your problem-solving.
```

---

## Emergency Scripts

```
If you blank out:
"Give me a moment to collect my thoughts..." (10 seconds is fine)

If problem is unclear:
"I want to make sure I understand correctly. When you say [X], 
do you mean [Y] or [Z]?"

If your solution has a bug:
"Ah, I see the issue. Let me fix that..." (don't panic, debug calmly)

If you realize a better approach mid-coding:
"Actually, I just realized there's a better approach. Would you like 
me to switch to that, or finish this one first?"

If you run out of time:
"I'm running low on time, but let me outline the remaining steps 
and the overall complexity..."
```
